WHERE  NEW YORK  TELLS  ITS  STORY  

.01

Statue of Liberty

An enduring symbol of freedom, the Statue of Liberty is a bucket-list attraction. Pair it with a visit to Ellis Island to explore the immigration museum and powerful stories of those who came to America seeking a better life.
Don’t Miss: The ferry ride offers stunning skyline views.

.02

Brooklyn Bridge

One of the most iconic walks in the city — connecting Manhattan and Brooklyn with sweeping views of the skyline, East River, and Statue of Liberty.
Don’t Miss: Explore DUMBO on the Brooklyn side for trendy shops, cafés, and photo spots.

.03

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

One of the world’s largest and finest art museums, the Met is home to over 2 million works spanning 5,000 years of history — from Egyptian relics to Van Gogh paintings.
Don’t Miss: The rooftop garden with seasonal exhibits and skyline views.

.04

Empire State Building

One of the most iconic skyscrapers in the world, the Empire State Building offers sweeping 360° views of New York City from its 86th and 102nd-floor observatories. A true architectural marvel and cinematic legend.
Don’t Miss: Visit just before sunset for golden hour views that turn into a sparkling skyline at night.

.05

Central Park

An urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan, Central Park offers beautiful trails, lakes, bridges, and landmarks. Ideal for walking, biking, picnicking, or even a carriage ride.
Don’t Miss: Bow Bridge, Bethesda Terrace, and Strawberry Fields.
