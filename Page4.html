SLICE OF THE CITY

Joe's Pizza

A cornerstone of New York’s pizza culture since 1975, Joe’s Pizza is the definition of a classic NYC slice joint. With its perfectly thin crust, gooey cheese, and a tangy tomato sauce that strikes a flawless balance, it’s no wonder both locals and celebrities make regular visits. Whether you’re grabbing a quick lunch or a midnight snack, this is the kind of slice you eat folded in half while walking through the streets of Manhattan — just like a real New Yorker.

One bite, and you’re a New Yorker.

John’s of Bleecker Street

Operating since 1929, John’s of Bleecker Street is a time capsule of pizza perfection. Using a coal-fired brick oven, they serve crispy, charred-crust pies with that distinctive old-world taste. You won’t find slices here — only whole pies that encourage you to sit down, share with friends, and savor every bite. The rustic setting, tiled floors, and decades of graffiti on the booths make the experience feel as historic as the recipe itself.

Where tradition meets taste—since 1929

Prince Street Pizza

This small pizzeria tucked away on Prince Street is world-famous for its bold, spicy SoHo Square — a thick Sicilian-style slice topped with crispy, cup-shaped pepperoni and bubbling mozzarella. The crust is crunchy on the outside, pillowy on the inside, and perfectly soaks in the rich tomato sauce. Expect long lines, especially at night — but the first bite proves it’s worth the wait. Prince Street Pizza is where NYC spice meets pizza perfection.

The slice that bites back.

Lombardi’s Pizza – Little Italy

Established in 1905, Lombardi’s is officially recognized as America’s first pizzeria — and it still delivers that authentic charm over a century later. The coal oven fires up beautifully blistered crusts, topped with fresh San Marzano tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and a drizzle of olive oil. Nestled in historic Little Italy, the ambiance makes you feel like you’ve stepped into pizza history. Every bite is steeped in heritage and flavor.

The original slice that started it all.

Scarr’s Pizza – Lower East Side

Scarr’s is a love letter to vintage NYC slice shops, but with a modern twist. They mill their own flour in-house, ensuring a unique crust with superior taste and texture. The interior rocks a retro 80s vibe, but the pizza is forward-thinking — organic, locally sourced, and outrageously flavorful. Whether it’s a cheese slice or a custom pie, Scarr’s is where old-school soul meets new-school standards.

Old-school flavor, new-school flair.

SWEET CITY BITE

Eileen’s Special Cheesecake – Nolita

An NYC legend since 1950, Junior’s delivers the ultimate New York-style cheesecake. Rich, creamy, and topped with their signature sponge cake crust, it’s a dessert staple that never goes out of style. With photos of celebrities lining the walls and a slice that melts in your mouth, it’s the kind of place where tradition feels delicious.

The World’s Most Fabulous Cheesecake.

Veniero’s Pasticceria & Caffè

A historic gem since 1894, Veniero’s brings an old-school Italian flair to cheesecake. Their ricotta version is smooth, slightly tangy, and lighter than traditional cream cheese varieties. Enjoy it in their gorgeous, chandelier-lit café, where every dessert tells a story of timeless tradition.

Where Italian tradition meets New York cheesecake.

Little Cupcake Bakeshop

Step into this charming, pastel-hued café that feels like a Pinterest dream. Their Brooklyn Blackout Cheesecake and classic NY-style slice are customer favorites — rich, indulgent, and Insta-worthy. The cozy vintage interiors and eco-conscious baking practices make this place a must-visit for dessert lovers with taste and style.

Where cheesecake meets charm.

Keki Modern Cakes – Chinatown

Home of the viral “bouncy” Japanese cheesecake, Keki offers a fresh and fluffy twist on the classic. Light as a cloud with a jiggly charm, their cakes are made with all-natural ingredients and baked in small batches. It’s part cheesecake, part soufflé — and totally addictive.

The cheesecake that wiggles and wows.

BREWED IN NYC

Stumptown Coffee Roasters

Known for its expertly roasted beans, Stumptown is a must-visit for coffee lovers who crave rich, flavorful brews. The intimate vibe and signature cold brew make this spot a favorite among locals and tourists alike. Whether you’re sipping on a classic espresso or trying a new pour-over, each cup is crafted to perfection.

Where every cup is a masterpiece.

Daily Provisions

A charming neighborhood café offering not only fantastic coffee but also delicious pastries. Daily Provisions is a true New York gem, with its modern yet cozy vibe. Whether you’re grabbing a latte or indulging in their renowned crullers, this spot is perfect for a laid-back coffee break.

Where every sip feels like home.

Cha Cha Matcha – Greenwich Village & SoHo

Cha Cha Matcha brings a fresh and fun twist to the coffee scene by mixing matcha with trendy, vibrant flavors. With its eye-catching pink walls and tropical décor, it’s as much a feast for the eyes as it is for your taste buds. Try their matcha lattes or a refreshing iced coconut matcha — it’s a whole vibe.

Sip the rainbow, live the vibe.

Ralph’s Coffee

For a truly chic coffee experience, Ralph’s Coffee serves up artisanal brews in the heart of Ralph Lauren’s flagship store. The café has an elegant, rustic charm that complements its refined coffee offerings. Enjoy a classic latte or cappuccino while you browse Ralph Lauren’s collection — it’s the perfect blend of style and taste.

Where coffee meets luxury.

Fellini Coffee – Lower East Side

Fellini Coffee is an artistic, European-style café tucked away in the Lower East Side, known for its romantic, cinematic interiors and moody ambiance. With beautifully brewed espressos and a carefully curated pastry selection, it’s the perfect hideaway for quiet moments or aesthetic Instagram snaps.

A little taste of Rome in downtown NYC.
